Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Grammar • A2

Objective: Understand when to use present simple (habits, facts) and present continuous (actions happening now).

Form and usage

Present simple

  • Form: Subject + base verb (add “s” for he/she/it). Example: “She works.”
  • Use: Habits, routines, facts, and schedules. Example: “The train leaves at 7.”

Present continuous

  • Form: Subject + am/is/are + verb‑ing. Example: “They are studying.”
  • Use: Actions happening now, temporary situations, and trends. Example: “I’m learning English.”
Tip: Signal words like “always/every day” often go with present simple; “now/currently” suggest present continuous.
Practice
  1. Fill the gap: She usually ___ (go) to work by bus.
  2. Transform: “He reads.” → Make it present continuous.
  3. Identify: Which tense fits? “Look! The dog ___ (run).”

Vocabulary: Daily routines

Vocabulary • A1

Objective: Learn essential daily routine verbs and collocations.

Word list
wake up get dressed have breakfast go to work come home go to bed

Example: “I wake up at 6 a.m. and have breakfast at 6:30.”

Practice
  1. Make a sentence with “have breakfast”.
  2. What time do you “go to bed” on weekdays?
  3. Create a short routine using at least three verbs.

Pronunciation: The “th” sound

Pronunciation • A2

Objective: Distinguish between /θ/ (think) and /ð/ (this) and practice minimal pairs.

How to position your tongue

Place the tip of your tongue lightly between your teeth. For /θ/ push air without voicing. For /ð/ add voice from the throat.

Minimal pairs
think / sink thin / tin then / den those / dose
